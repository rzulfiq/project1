Critical Discussion of Technology and Applications

Social Implications:

Ethical Lens 1 - Privacy Concerns:

AR/VR technologies often collect and use user data for personalization and tracking.
Concerns arise regarding data privacy, consent, and potential misuse of personal information.
AR/VR companies may monetize user data through targeted advertising or other means, illustrating how companies can leverage user data for financial gain.

Ethical Lens 2 - Inclusivity and Accessibility:

VR headsets, in particular, may exclude individuals with certain disabilities, such as motor function limitations or sensory limitations.
Ensuring inclusive design and accessibility features remains a challenge in AR/VR development.
Cultural and socioeconomic factors also contribute to the accessibility challenges of AR/VR technologies.

Ethical Lens 3 - User Health and Safety Recommendations:

Prolonged use of VR can lead to physical discomfort, eye strains, headaches, and potentially contribute to mental health issues, such as addiction or disorientation.
Design choices should aim to create engaging experiences without causing harm or distress to users.
